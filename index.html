Here's Why You should Use Secure Trade

Major benefits for both buyers and sellers involved in transactions.

Security

It provides a neutral third-party intermediary that securely holds funds until both parties fulfill their obligations. This reduces the risk of fraud or loss for both the buyer and the seller.

Dispute Resolution

If there's a disagreement between the buyer and seller, the escrow service can step in to mediate and help resolve the issue. This can prevent costly legal battles and help facilitate a fair resolution.

International Transactions

Escrow services are particularly beneficial for international transactions, where there may be additional risks due to currency exchange rates, shipping, and regulatory differences. Escrow services can help mitigate these risks and ensure a smoother transaction process.

Payment Verification

Escrow services can verify the legitimacy of funds before releasing them to the seller. This helps prevent instances of payment fraud or insufficient funds.

How It Works?

Step 1: Agreement

The buyer and seller agree to the terms of the transaction, including the description of the goods or services, price, and any other relevant details. They also agree to use an escrow service to facilitate the transaction.

Step 2: Initiation

The buyer initiates the transaction by submitting payment to the escrow service. This payment is typically held securely in an escrow account until the transaction is completed.

Step 3: Verification

The escrow service verifies the payment and the terms of the transaction. This may involve confirming the buyer's payment method, identity verification, and reviewing the transaction details.

Step 4: Fulfillment

Once the payment is verified, the seller proceeds to deliver the goods or provide the agreed-upon services to the buyer. This step is crucial, as it ensures that the seller fulfills their obligations as per the transaction agreement.

Step 5: Acceptance

Upon receiving the goods or services, the buyer has the opportunity to inspect them to ensure they meet the agreed-upon standards. If everything is satisfactory, the buyer accepts the delivery or services.

Step 6: Funds Release

Once the buyer accepts the goods or services, the escrow service releases the funds to the seller. This step ensures that the seller receives payment only after fulfilling their obligations and the buyer's satisfaction.

Step 7: Completion

With the transaction concluded, both parties may provide feedback or ratings on their experience with the escrow service and each other. This feedback helps build trust within the community and enhances the reputation of both buyers and sellers.

Step 8: Dispute Resolution

In the event of a dispute or disagreement between the buyer and seller, the escrow service may step in to mediate and facilitate a resolution. This could involve reviewing documentation, communication records, and other evidence to reach a fair outcome.
